# Slide 1: Kill The Clipboard

Generate one full-slide 16:9 presentation image.

## Exact Title

`Kill The Clipboard`

## Exact Subtitle

`Share selected clinical history, insurance card, and your Patient Story from your app to your care team — no portal sign-in required.`

## Layout

White canvas, strong top-left title. Under title, a clean before/after flow that tells the full project story in miniature:

1. Left: a simple clipboard / intake form labeled `Repeated intake basics`.
2. Center-left: a patient app on phone titled `You choose what to share`. Include short checklist labels: Problems / concerns, Medications, Allergies, Immunizations, Insurance card, Patient Story.
3. Center-right: a clean card titled `Secure QR / link`. Put a large QR-code-style placeholder centered in a reserved box above the label `Secure packet`. Do not try to make it scannable. Add tiny labels: `Encrypted`, `Patient controlled`, `Time-limited`.
4. Right: `Care Team EHR`. Make all text fit in the card. Show received artifacts inside the EHR: `FHIR summary`, `Coverage information`, `Patient Story PDF`. Add small line: `Arrives before or during check-in.`

Use arrows between stages. Keep the graphic calm, not busy. Put a small footer reference only: `ktc-spec.github.io`.

## Takeaway Line

No large slogan band. If a concise bottom line is needed for balance, use exactly: `Reusable basics can move from a patient app to the care team's system.`

## Avoid

- Do not say "inbox".
- Do not use the phrase "stop retyping".
- Do not imply the QR is the clinical goal.
- Do not show `shlink://`.
