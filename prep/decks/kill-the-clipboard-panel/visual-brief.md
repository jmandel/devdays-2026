# Visual Brief: Kill the Clipboard Panel Intro

## Overall Direction

This is a short setup deck for a live panel discussion. It should feel concrete, candid, and clinically grounded, not like a product launch. The repeated visual grammar is a clean white health-tech diagram style: large readable titles, thin colored linework, rounded workflow cards, familiar healthcare artifacts, and one teal lower emphasis band where useful.

The emotional arc is: the whole KTC premise -> current reusable basics -> what still needs future check-in -> practical discussion.

## Style References

- Use the displayed SMART deck slide titled "Launch is SMART/OAuth setup for an app session" as the typography/header reference: broad heavy clean sans-serif title, top-left placement, generous margins, strong readable body text, thin colored linework, disciplined white space, and occasional teal lower takeaway band.
- Use the user-provided SMART Evolution timeline only as broad palette inspiration: sky blue, teal, orange, green, purple, red, and gold on a white background. Do not copy the SMART logo, star mark, or timeline layout unless a slide explicitly asks for a timeline.
- Use the user-provided "Kill The Clipboard: July 2026 Milestone" explainer only as background grounding for artifact categories and accessible patient/clinician language: selected sharing, QR/link, EHR retention, PAMI, Coverage, Patient Story, optional pre-visit SMART Health Link, and the distinction between current progress and future request-specific check-in. Do not reproduce the busy layout, do not make the deck a July milestone explainer, and do not overfit to April/July chronology.
- Use the KTC/SMART Health Check-in source material for factual grounding. Do not invent product UI details, fake CMS logos, fake EHR vendor logos, fake app names, or fake screenshots.

## Typography And Layout

- Titles: broad, non-condensed, bold sans-serif. Never horizontally squeeze text. Split long titles into title plus subtitle.
- Body text: plain sans-serif, high contrast, readable from the back of the room.
- Use short labels and artifact chips instead of paragraphs.
- Use monospaced text only for URLs, FHIR resources, and repo names.
- Keep a consistent 16:9 canvas and request 1920x1080 generation.
- Avoid recurring logos. A single first-slide title treatment is enough.
- Footer/reference treatment may use `ktc-spec.github.io` when useful. Do not create a separate reference implementation slide in this short version.

## Visual Motifs

- Clipboard/paper fields as repeated basics.
- Patient app/wallet/PHR choosing and annotating.
- FHIR bundle / SMART Health Link / QR as transport artifacts.
- Care Team EHR receiving retained, labeled, reviewable patient-shared content.
- Discussion setup as substantive question cards, not portraits or biographies.

## Guardrails

- Do not imply "launch" means opening a portal; this deck is not about SMART App Launch mechanics except where health app data is involved.
- Do not use clever metaphors like failed databases or decentralized databases. Keep the deck plain and explanatory.
- Do not make the patient app magic. Show patient review, data source provenance, and optional annotation.
- Do not turn Patient Story into decorative text. It is a clinical artifact with patient voice.
- Do not imply CMS has mandated all behavior. Keep voluntary ecosystem and future July 2026 target language precise.
- Do not draw every slide as a generic cloud diagram.
- Do not use robots, AI brains, sparkles, generic stock patients, fake vendor logos, or fake government seals.
- Do not overload slides with spec citations; keep citations and detail in notes/interview prep.
